Stop, Question, and Frisk:
An analysis of NYPD policing strategy with Python

This is a Python project that takes us into the NYPD Stop, Question and Frisk (SQF) strategy. As approved by the U.S Supreme court in 1968, officers were granted limited rights to stop, question and frisk persons who they believed to be dangerous. What would later happen was a series of controversies, as many believed the program went on to become unfair with huge cases of racial-profiling and intimidation reported.

This report focuses on examining the data gathered from the program for the year 2012 as it was in this year that the people began to speak up and protests greeted the world. The dataset seeks to uncover interesting biases as well as review information based on the reported claims.

Four parts of Python codes were written for this project, to read the CSV file that contains the 2012 SQF dataset and perform Association rule mining, Cluster Analysis and Predictive Modelling.
